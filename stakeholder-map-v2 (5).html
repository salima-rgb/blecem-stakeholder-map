Community Engagement Action Plan

Recommended engagement strategies per quadrant, with specific tasks, cadence guidance and candid sensitivity notes for BL leadership. This plan should be reviewed quarterly and updated as relationships evolve.

⚠ Sensitivity Notes: Direct & Candid — Named Risks for BL Leadership
Collaborate — High Influence, High Interest
Quarterly steering · Monthly 1:1s · Co-design at every stage
▲
Engagement Strategy
These stakeholders must be co-designers, not consultees. Any sense of being informed after decisions are made will damage relationships irreparably — particularly with STCA, faith leaders and resident associations. Full co-design from the earliest possible stage is non-negotiable.

⚠ BL Leadership Note

The risk of institutional capture is real. If BL leadership drives the agenda and community groups are brought in to validate rather than shape decisions, this project will face active opposition. Camden has a long memory. Previous developments in Somers Town have generated lasting distrust. The community engagement budget and timeline must reflect genuine co-production, not consultation theatre. This means community groups having real input into programming, hiring, and space design — not just being asked for feedback on a finished plan.

    Keep Satisfied — High Influence, Low Interest
    Milestone briefings · Formal reporting · Early warning system
    ▼
    Engagement Strategy
    These stakeholders hold significant power over the project's success — planning approvals, funding, infrastructure — but are not primarily driven by community outcomes. Keep them satisfied by demonstrating rigorous process, strong social value evidence, and proactive communication at key milestones. Never let them hear bad news first from the media or community complaints.

    ⚠ BL Leadership Note

    Historic England and C20 Society are significant risks on architectural and heritage grounds — they will scrutinise the extension's impact on the Grade I listed building and the Euston Road streetscape. Any design concession that appears to compromise heritage must be stress-tested against their likely response before it reaches planning. HS2 has already strained community trust in the Somers Town area — BL must clearly differentiate this project as community-positive and actively coordinate on communications to avoid conflation.

      Keep Informed — Low Influence, High Interest
      Regular updates · Multilingual comms · Accessible consultation
      ▼
      Engagement Strategy
      This is the largest and most diverse group — residents, schools, faith communities, TRAs, diverse community organisations. They have the highest stake in the project's outcomes and the greatest risk of being sidelined. Proactive, accessible, and multilingual communication is essential. Updates must reach people before decisions are made, not after. Community newsletter, open meetings, social media, and peer-to-peer outreach through trusted intermediaries (STCA, faith leaders, housing associations).

      ⚠ BL Leadership Note

      Somers Town has a high proportion of Bangladeshi, Somali, and East African communities for whom English may not be a first language, and who have historically been excluded from institutional consultation processes. If the engagement approach defaults to English-language written consultation (letters, surveys, online forms), it will miss a significant portion of the community most affected by the development. This is not just an equity issue — it is a reputational and planning risk. Budget for translation, community liaison workers, and in-person outreach from the outset.

      Engagement Cadence
      Monthly E-newsletter Termly Community Meetings Social Media Updates School Outreach (Termly) Faith Network Briefings TRA Meetings

        Monitor — Low Influence, Low Interest
        Watch for narrative shifts · Engage at milestones only
        ▼
        Engagement Strategy
        Monitor this group for any shifts in interest or influence that might require escalation. Engage only at major milestones. Ensure the communications team is equipped to respond if media narrative turns negative.

        ⚠ BL Leadership Note

        Hotel and commercial interests on Ossulston Street and surrounding areas could become active opponents if construction disruption significantly impacts their operations. Proactive coordination on construction timelines and access will be cheaper than managing a complaint campaign. Do not underestimate the Pullman and Renaissance hotels' capacity to organise if they feel inadequately consulted.

        Watch Triggers — Escalate if:
        • Negative media coverage emerges around displacement or heritage
        • Hotel/commercial operators raise formal planning objections
        • Local politicians begin raising constituent concerns publicly
        • Social media campaigns against the project emerge
